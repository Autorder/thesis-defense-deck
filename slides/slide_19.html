From insights to design rules

Insight 1
PV is dominant

Panel area drives both independence and savings — more than storage, wind, or degradation.

Design rulePV first — maximize the roof before expanding storage.
Insight 2
Storage shifts, it does not generate

A battery makes no new energy — it moves midday surplus into the evening. There is only so much surplus to move, so most of the benefit lands by 10–15 kWh. Wind pays off only where the resource is strong.

Design ruleStorage 10–15 kWh; add turbines only in windy sites (e.g. Haifa).
Insight 3
A regulatory gap

The solar system pays for itself; the battery is a resilience expense, not a returning investment. That is a tariff outcome — today’s rates reward selling surplus, not storing it.

Policy ruleDynamic tariffs that price distributed storage — to back the 30%-by-2030 target.
The technology is mature — what lags is the incentive structure. Storage economics is a question of regulation, not just technology.
